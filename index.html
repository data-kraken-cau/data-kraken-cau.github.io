Welcome to KRAKEN 2025

The KRAKEN 2025 conference covers the bachelor and master seminar of the working group Archeoinformatics - Data Science. You as a student will receive one of the topics listed below with the goal to read, interpret and present the paper and its idea to other students in a conference talk and to us and student reviewers in a written paper.

Topics

Bachelor seminar:

  • Skyline Routing
  • Heterogenous Information Networks
  • Spatial Knowlegde Graphs
  • Statistical Traffic Prediction

Master seminar:

  • CNN + LSTM for Traffic Prediction
  • Graph Convolutional Layer
  • Diffusion Convolution with Recurrent Neural Networks
  • Graph Attention Networks
  • Self Adaptive Adjancency Matrices
  • Sparse Traffic Observations

Call for Papers

We invite papers discussing the topic of their anchor paper and compare the ideas and results to those of the paper pool. For bachelor students each supervisor will provide one additional paper to which you have to find a second paper as you paper pool. For master students you will only be provided with the anchor paper and you have to find two more related papers. Paper pool papers can provide a different view on the topic, a different approach to the problem or a different application of the method. The goal is to find a common ground between the papers and to discuss the differences and similarities.

Submission Guidelines

Submitted papers must be maximum 6 pages long (including all figures, tables, and references). Submissions should be formatted using the overleaf template. All abstracts and papers must be submitted using the Microsoft CMT portal:

  • Submission via CMT
